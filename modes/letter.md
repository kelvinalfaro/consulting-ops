# Cover letter or letter of interest

Create a solicitation-specific letter, never a generic placeholder.

## Gates and inputs

1. Require a captured opportunity, authoritative solicitation, confirmed letter requirement or strategic purpose, permitted recipient/channel, and due date. If any are missing, stop and ask for them.
2. Load only approved sources: company profile, capability statement, relevant case studies, team bios, writing samples, custom/profile modes, the solicitation and amendments, and current user facts.
3. Extract the issuer's exact need, evaluation language, requested outcomes, scope boundaries, and 5-10 phrases worth mirroring. Separate source language from interpretation.
4. Research recent issuer context only from authoritative sources. Show the short source-linked synthesis to the user and ask them to confirm or correct it.
5. Identify evidence gaps, conflicts, lobbying/contact restrictions, signer authority, and any claim that needs permission.

## Required user choices

Before final prose, ask for: the pursuit angle; the problem the firm should emphasize; the opening approach or differentiator; the desired tone; and the authorized signer. Offer evidence-backed options, but do not choose silently.

## Draft contract

Use `npx consulting-ops letter <opportunity.yml>` for the workspace. Draft 300-500 words unless the solicitation specifies otherwise: issuer-specific opening; concise understanding of need; 2-4 supported qualifications or proof points; a bounded approach/value paragraph; and a direct close. Every material claim must map to an approved source. Avoid generic enthusiasm, invented metrics, unsupported client names, new scope promises, pricing, legal acceptance, or certifications.

Present the complete letter for review before export. Revise until the user approves the text. Never send, sign, or represent that the signer approved it.

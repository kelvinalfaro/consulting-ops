# Standalone workflow fallbacks

Use these fallbacks only when the named optional skill is unavailable. They keep the public concierge useful without pretending that Consulting Ops contains a full engagement-management or financial-analysis engine.

## Shape an inquiry

Capture the sponsor, governing question, intended outcomes, decision owner, stakeholders, scope boundaries, constraints, timing, available evidence, and unresolved decisions. Separate confirmed facts from assumptions. Produce a short engagement-shaping brief and recommend whether the next step is discovery, a proposal/SOW, an RFP pursuit, or no action.

## Shape a standalone proposal or SOW

Confirm the governing question, outcomes, scope, exclusions, deliverables, roles, decision rights, milestones, dependencies, assumptions, client responsibilities, commercial model, and acceptance criteria. Keep pricing, legal terms, and commitment language under explicit human review. Do not use the RFP proposal command unless an authoritative solicitation record exists.

## Steward an active engagement

Compare the accepted charter with current delivery. Identify evidence of drift, unresolved decisions, sponsor or working-team gaps, dependencies, risks, and the smallest reset needed. Prepare a sponsor conversation, decision list, handoff, or closeout record without changing scope or commitments automatically.

## Bound non-core analysis

Inventory the supplied files, identify their types and periods, and state the analytical question. If a suitable spreadsheet, document, or financial-analysis capability is available, use it. Otherwise stop after a source-faithful intake and explain that Consulting Ops does not itself calculate CSV summaries, financial performance, ROI, NPV, or sensitivity analysis. Never infer missing figures.

## Draft a bounded client communication

Draft from the authoritative engagement or pursuit record. Lead with the supported answer, decision, request, or next action; distinguish facts, judgments, open questions, and approvals. Do not send the message, accept terms, approve pricing, or make commitments on the user's behalf.
